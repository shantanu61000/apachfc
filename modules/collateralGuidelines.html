Collateral Norms - Legal & Technical - AP

Collateral is an asset  that a lender accepts as security for extending a loan. If the borrower defaults on her loan payments, the lender may seize the collateral and sell it to recoup some or all his losses. Collateral is an item of value that is accepted as security for a loan.

Particulars Description
Property Type
  • Residential/Commercial property owned and occupied by the borrowers
  • Rented Residential / Commercial properties which are partly rented and partly self-occupied. The self-occupancy of such properties to be minimal of 60% of the property.
Title Search and Valuation Search Loan Amount <= 8 Lakhs
  • Title Search to be undertaken by empanelled lawyer approved by APAC HFC/NBFC.
  • Valuation will be done by one independent empanelled valuer approved by APAC HFC/NBFC.
Loan Amount > 8 Lakhs to <= to 35 Lakhs
  • Title Search & Valuation to be undertaken by empanelled lawyer approved by APAC HFC/NBFC.
  • Valuation will be done by 2 independent empanelled valuers approved by APAC HFC/NBFC.
Security Registered Mortgage
Collateral - Chain link document & EC:

Latest title deed / gift deed / partition deed / document showing ownership in original should be available. In case of purchased property, at least 12 months should have lapsed from the date of registered document.

In case of ancestral /family property, records to show the possession of 13 years is to be obtained along with one title document.

First Level: Credit Officer/Credit Manager takes the online EC from (Government website - http://registration.ap.gov.in/) and verifies the online EC; After CO/CM signs the online EC report printout it is submitted to empanelled advocate for TSR & vetting reports purposes.

Second Level: Empanelled advocate will again independently verify the online EC in system and Sign and stamp the online EC report. The Online EC details to be captured in TSR & Vetting reports by empanelled advocate.

The following steps should be ensured in order to create a valid Mortgage:
  1. Title Search Report from empanelled Lawyer should have clear title in respect to the property offered as collateral.
  2. All original property documents as mentioned in the Title Search Report to be taken from the customer in original before disbursement.
  3. The Registered deed to be signed by the customer and officials of APAC who receives the property documents.
  4. Empanelled lawyer providing the title search report for the property offered as a collateral should visit the branch and check the original property documents submitted by the customer / Our officer visit to lawyer office and shows the original property documents, after check the original property documents submitted by the officer.
  5. Empanelled lawyer to provide clear vetting report for the original property documents verified.
  6. All the taxes to be paid at the time of mortgaging the property. The same to verified by the lawyer and latest tax receipt to be attached with legal report.
  7. If latest construction house, if customer has not received the house tax demand, we need to collect the possession certificate from Panchayat.
  8. Internal legal clearance as and when needed.
  9. The original property documents of the applicant should be sealed as per the order mentioned in the title search report and should be sent to Central OPs.
Valuer observes the below points at the time of valuation:
  • The size of property and land
  • The number and type of rooms
  • The fixtures and fittings
  • The stucture and condition of the building
  • Check the property boundaries
  • Ease of access to the property
  • He observes any objection from local body
  • The property's location and level of amenities
  • He checks road width and way of property as per document
  • He can also observe as document and actual extent
  • He checks and verifiy the market values

  • Minor properties
  • Already assigned mortgage properties
  • Unregistered partition deed after 1985 cases not acceptable
  • Unregistered will also not acceptable
  • GPA Properties (Agreement sale cum GPA)
  • Which property documents is not registerable that properties is also not acceptable
