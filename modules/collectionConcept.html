DPD Calculation:

If Keith makes payment toward his January EMI anytime during the month, his loan account will move back to 0 DPD

But if he doesn't:
On 31st January, his loan account shall be 27 DPD
On 4th February, his loan account would be 31 DPD

At this time, the Feb EMI is also due.
If he bounces the Feb EMI the DPD continues to increase by 1 each day.

However, if he clears the Feb EMI:
it shall go towards setting off the January emi

Why ..?

Because: The rule is, any payment received is first updated towards the earliest EMI.

When the Feb EMI clears, and it gets adjusted towards January dues, what effectively remains due now is the February EMI

Overdues:

'Overdues' are the amounts due but not paid. In other words, overdue are sum of all the EMIs that have d=fallen due till now but unpaid

The sum of inerest portion in these EMIs would be the overdue interest.

The sum of principal portion in these EMIs would be overdue principal

NPA and Provision

NPA:

Loan amounts are "Assets" for a company because the generate income in the form of interest

However, when a customer defaults their EMI, htere us no interest earned. Not only that, their principal amount is also at a high risk of default.

When such an account remains unpaid for more than 90 days (i.e More than 90 DPD), it is called a "Non Performing Asset"

Provision for Loss:

We are required to make a "provision for loss" on every account that is classified ad NPA.
A Provision is made by setting aside a portion from the profits.

We Know that:
Profit = Income - Expenses

If a provision has to be made, then:
Profit = Income - Expenses - Provision for loss

Thus, provision for loss has the effect of reducing our profits

The amount of provision is decided by the NPA category to which the loan is classified. For NPAs that have DPDs, a higher percentage is set aside as a provision.
