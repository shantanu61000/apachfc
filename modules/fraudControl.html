Document Fraud-Types

Tittle Document Fraud
  • Creating multiple fake documents of the same property and depositing in multiple institutions.
  • Employee and Customer submitted fake patta and availed loan
  • Borrower used fake builder NOC and demand letter to take loan.
Critical Document Fraud
  • The Salary Slip was forged by the borrower.
  • Income documents, ID proofs & property documents submitted by customer were forged. Outside parties viz. DSA legal technical vendors may have colluded with the customer.
  • The borrower has produced forged PAN cards and IT Returns.
Dummy Borrower / Impersonation
  • Builder selling to his contractor/ employees and doing subsequent resale.
Disbursement to fake/forged third party account
  • The borrower managed to open bank account in seller’s name by impersonating him and got the disbursement cheque cleared through the said account.
Own Contribution Money
  • OCR was transferred by seller through some other persons account to suppress the clear trail. Customer fabricated the narration of OCR transfer in banks statement to hide source of funds.
  • Seller being a friend of customer transferred OCR through some Amrendera Kumar’s account to customer’s account. To suppress the clear trail, customer fabricated the narrations of OCR transfer in bank statement to hide source of funds.
Loan Documents not signed executed properly, mortgage not created property
  • Property not mortgaged as intimation of Mortgage was forged and was not registered.
Inflated Valution
  • Borrower Misguided the inspecting official of the Company by showing some other house under construction.

Bank Statement – Red Flags

  • Transactions should be within the period for which the statement has been printed.
  • Any transaction BEFORE or AFTER that date means the Bank statements have been fudged !!
  • Transactions should not be present on Bank Holidays . Dates to watch are 29th Feb (in non – leap years); 26th Jan, 15 Aug, 2nd Oct; Holi, Diwali, 31 Dec, Christmas, Id-ul-Fitar etc.,Transactions should be in ASCENDING order of dates.
  • The Interest paid by the bank is always Credited periodically (3 months generally Dec end/Jan, April beginning, June end/ July, Sep end/ Oct Or 2wice a year –Jun end / July + Dec end / Jan.
  • Cheque numbers should not be in series generally (until he is using cheques only to pay a monthly loan).
  • Cheque numbers should not be in series generally (until he is using cheques only to pay a monthly loan).
  • The newly opened account can be determined if the first balance in the statement is Zero.
  • Check the totals, incase of addition mistakes , the statement is fudged.
  • Most public sector banks use a dot matrix printer. Pvt banks like ICICI, HDFC etc., uses Laser printer.
  • Any discrepancy in the Font in the body of the statement and the heading (name of customer, account no etc) means that statement is fudged.
  • Minimum balance charges need to be Debited from the account.
