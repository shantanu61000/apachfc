LTV Loan to Value

Gita and Sita are good friends. Both share a taste for fine leather accessories.
They request Rita to lend them Rs.10000 so as to shop from a neighborhood store. They promise to repay the money in a month’s time when they get their allowance.

Aunt Rita obliges with the condition that if they fail to repay within a month, she shall take away all their purchases.
Gita and Sita agreed to the condition and used the money to shop for shoes and bags.

In the course of the month, a lot of new leather products’ shops open up in the area. The market becomes more competitive. Now, those same shoes and bags are available at cheaper rates.

Gita and Sita get their allowance for the month. They ponder over for some time and decide, instead of repaying , it is better to let her take away the items purchased last month. Since the goods are cheaper now, they could buy more items with their allowance money instead of repaying their debt to Aunt Rita

Gita and Sita take off shopping once again, while Aunt Rita is left with shoes and bags that are worth much lower than Rs.10000 at which they were originally purchased!

What happened to Aunt Rita could happen to any lender who provides loan against a secured asset !!!!!!!!!!!!...

The biggest risk in a lending business is the risk of default

Hence, various credit checks are done on a customer before issuing a loan.

Yet, it may just so happen that even though the customer can afford to pay and also considering that they may be well intentioned otherwise,
They may still decide to default as repaying the loan does not make financial sense.

How do we ensure that we do not fall victim to such a scenario?

By lending only to the extent we think the value could be ‘REALIZED’ from the asset in future. In other words, by leaving a portion of the asset unfinanced.


Aunt Rita would have been better off today, had she had lent say Rs.6000 or so for items worth Rs.10000. That way Gita and Sita would bear a portion of the cost and would be wary of defaulting on the loan and losing their goods to Aunt Jenny. They would have more likely repaid the debt.

Agreement Value in layman terms can be explained as the value on which two parties have decided to execute the transaction of the property. Agreement value is the value that is shown on the sale deed or on which the loan amount is agreed.

Acquiring a property in India market value means it is the price on which the property is being sold in the open market. Generally it is considered that Market value is always higher than the agreement value.

A distressed sale occurs when a property, stock, or another asset must be sold quickly. Distressed sales often result in a financial loss for the seller who, for reasons of economic duress, must accept a lower price.

Mortgages

Mortgage is a means of securing some financial interest.

Mortgages can be of different kinds
Financial Interest can also be of various kinds For the purpose of understanding, we shall concern ourselves with mortgage on house properties

How does a mortgage work ..?

Let’s say, A advances a loan of Rs. 5 lacs to B. He puts a condition that if B doesn’t pay up, he shall have a right to recover the loan by selling B’s House. Here, B is said to have mortgaged his house to A. The loan of Rs.5 Lac is A’s ‘financial interest’ which he has secured by such an arrangement.The borrower or person who mortgages the property is called ‘mortgagor’ and the lender or person in whose favor the mortgage is made is called ‘mortgagee’. Thus, in the above example, B is the mortgagor and A is the mortgagee Mortgage on house property is principally of two kinds :

  1. Registered Mortgage
  2. Equitable Mortgage
Type of Mortgages
Registered Mortgages

In the above example, A and B may decide to enter into a formal contract for mortgage involving following

  • ‘Mortgage deed’ would be drafted with terms of mortgage clearly specified. - requiring a meticulous legal process
  • Terms of mortgage would typically include details like loan value, what would constitute a default, when and how the mortgage would be enforced, details of the property, etc. - time consuming
  • Document would be stamped by paying a stamp duty fee and the mortgage is registered. Registering a mortgage deed ensures that mortgage reflects against the said property in government records. Title to the property would therefore not be clear until the mortgage is released. This ensures protection to the lender as well as a prospective buyer. – Stamp duty & Registration fee is expensive
  • Unviable especially for housing finance companies where large volumes of transactions are involved.
Equitable Mortgage
  • Equitable mortgage is a simple process of creating a mortgage by depositing original title deeds of the property with the mortgagee.
  • Therefore, with a simple act of depositing the original property papers with Memorandom of Entry A, B is said to have mortgaged his property to A.
  • The memorandcum of entry primarily states that an equitable mortgage has been, created. Thus, the original property papers have to be deposited first before the, MOE process can be initiated.
  • The process and stepsgit involved differs in different locations pan India. Broadly,the memorandum specifies details like: date of deposit, place of deposit, detailsof the loan, etc.

CIBIL Score

Your CIBIL score or credit score is an important factor in determining whether you will be approved for a loan or not. CIBIL scores are generated based on your credit history which includes past credit taken and payment patterns in relation to them. 

CIBIL was abbreviation for Credit Information Bureau of India Limited. It is one of the “credit bureaus” or “credit information company” which provides credit scores for an Individual consumer in India. The credit scores are in the range of 300–900.

GOOD CIBIL SCORE

The CIBIL credit score ranges from 300 to 900. The closer you are to 900, the more confidence the credit institution will have in your ability to pay the loan and hence, the better the chances of your application getting approved. Anything above 750 is considered a good credit score. All banks /NBFCs usually look at the credit score as one one of the manu checks they do before advancing a loan.

FICO SCORE / CREDIT SCORE

FICO - Fair Isaac Corporation is a major analytics software company that provides products and services to both businesses and consumers. Previously known as the Fair Isaac Corporation, the company changed its name to FICO in 2009 and is best known for producing the most widely used consumer credit scores that financial institutions use in deciding whether to lend money or issue credit. FICO scores range between 300 and 850. In general, scores above 650 indicate a very good credit history . The most common credit score is the FICO Score which is calculated using five main categories of credit data from your credit reports. Here they are, along with what percent of the score they account for.

WHAT IS PAYMENT HISTORY

Your payment history is a record of on-time, late and missed payments on past and current credit accounts. These accounts can include credit cards, lines of credit, personal loans and mortgages

CIBIL Score is a 3 digit numeric summary of your credit history, derived by using details found in the 'Accounts' and 'Enquiries' sections on your CIBIL Report and ranges from 300 to 900. The closer your score is to 900, the higher are the chances of your loan application getting approved

5 MAIN CREDIT CATEGORY

  • Payment History (35%). Whether you’ve paid your past bills on time
  • Amounts Owed (30%). How much credit and how many loans you’re using
  • Length of Credit History (15%). How long you’ve had credit
  • Credit Mix (10%). The types of credit you’ve had (e.g., mortgage, auto loan, credit cards)
  • New Credit (10%). Frequency of credit inquires and new account openings

MAIN OBSERVATION IN CIBIL

CIBIL score 0 means that information about the borrower's credit history is available only up to a period of less than 6 months in total.... 

CIBIL score -1 means that no information about the borrower's credit history whatsoever. There is no information to report, hence this score is also known as “NH” or “no history”.

Standard (STD): Payments being made within 90 days.

Special Mention Account (SMA): Special account created for reporting Standard Accounts moving toward Sub-Standard.

Sub-Standard (SUB): Payments being made after 90 days.

Doubtful (DBT): The account has remained Sub-Standard for 12 months

Loss – LSS: an account where loss has been identified and remains uncollectible

CLOSED / SETTLED / WRITTEN OFF

Closed: If you find a date adjacent to the 'Closed' field in your account section, this means that that loan account has been closed by the lender. In other words, it means you have paid off your loan in full and the bank has reported this account as "Closed" to CIBIL.

After closing a loan it is important to obtain a No Dues Certificate (NDC) from the lender, banks issue a No Due Certificate (NDC) or Closure Letter while closing loans stating that the loan stands closed ..

Settled: If you have partly paid the dues and settled a loan or credit card then the status will reflect as "Settled" in your credit report. When you settle an account, it means that the credit institution is agreeing to accept a payoff amount that is less than the amount originally owed. Because the lending institution is taking a loss, a status of "settled" may be considered potentially negative and detrimental to the chances of loan approval

Written-off: When you are not able to make payments against the outstanding loan/credit card amount for more than 180 days, the lender is required to "write-off" the amount in question. The lender then proceeds to report this on your CIBIL Report as "Written off". This is a detrimental status for the approval of your loan or credit card applications as the lender may not want to provide a loan or credit card to someone who has not paid dues on past loans or credit cards. If the CIBIL Report shows a "settled" or "written off" status, then it may get difficult for the individual to obtain a loan.
